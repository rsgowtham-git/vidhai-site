Where AI Takes Root — விதை ("seed" in Tamil)

Our Story

Vidhai (விதை) means "seed" in Tamil — a small beginning with the potential to reshape entire systems, and a word that also evokes knowledge and insight in everyday Tamil usage. Vidhai is also a deliberate play on Vidh + AI: Vidh echoing a family of words around learning and knowledge, and AI standing for artificial intelligence — the technology now amplifying how we think, build, and work. Vidhai at vidhai.co is an independent AI news and semiconductor insights platform, not affiliated with any other organization using the name, built to help professionals make sense of an AI world that's moving faster than most industries can adapt.

I grew up in the age of landlines, cassette tapes, and internet cafés where a single web page could take minutes to load over a noisy dial‑up modem. Two decades later, after working across automotive, industrial, hi‑tech, consumer products, medical devices, and energy, I found myself at the intersection of product engineering, manufacturing, and AI—wanting a single place that connected AI hype to the realities of the shop floor and the supply chain.

Vidhai is my attempt to plant that seed. I founded it after 20+ years in sales, account management, and consultative roles, helping teams turn complex engineering problems into practical, scalable solutions and seeing firsthand how technology, process, and people either work together—or get in each other's way. The platform curates AI news, semiconductor industry moves, training resources, and practical AI tools, always through the lens of someone who understands both traditional operations and the transformative potential of artificial intelligence.

I'm especially interested in the messy middle where business strategy meets real‑world constraints—legacy systems, disconnected data, tribal knowledge, and change‑fatigued teams—and Vidhai reflects that bias toward the real world over buzzwords. This is my way of thinking in public: exploring how AI, chips, and automation are reshaping work, and making that knowledge accessible to professionals transitioning into the AI era, whether they come from manufacturing, engineering, business, or any other domain.

I live in the SF Bay Area with my family, still very much that kid who wants to press every button, peek behind every panel, and figure out how things really work—only now the systems are bigger, the stakes are higher, and Vidhai is the seed I'm nurturing along the way.
